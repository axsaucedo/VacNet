Why?

Currently, vaccination programme is one of the immunisation services that is widely being carried out all across the globe as a mean of protecting children against viral diseases that may result in permanent health instability. Thus, in order to keep our progress aligned towards creating a healthy environment for the younger generation, it is crucial to establish a chain of activity that helps to promote a better treatment solution by eradicating any unwanted activities that may jeopardize the whole objective of this service. According to UNICEF, "one-fifth of the world’s children – about 22.4 million infants – are not immunized against these killer diseases. More than 70 percent of these children live in ten countries. An estimated 1.5 million children died in 2011 from vaccine-preventable diseases. The deadlines for eliminating maternal and neonatal tetanus and certification of global polio eradication by 2010 have not been met." We believe VacTrack can act as a viable solution to help save childrens from such diseases.

What?

VacTrack is currently available as both mobile and Web-based application whereby it can be accessed on devices that runs on any platform due to the universality that Web technology offers us today. Its uniqueness in accepting children's biometric prints allows a systematic way of utilizing the vaccines that has been supplied to each care center besides eliminating the risks of any form of fraudulent activities that may act as a barrier for a child from being treated especially cases of vaccines being sold to any third party pharmaceuticals for their personal gain. Furthermore, regular data sync between local and the cloud storage allows both the organization and health institutions to keep a consistent record on statistics of childs being treated miles away besides eliminating the conventional archiving system which requires a great amount of effort and large amount of documents on shelves.

How?

The process is simple where it takes only few steps to accomplish the objective. An organization who supplies the vaccines and the care centers that receives it will be given the privilege to operate this system so as to make sure that the supply meets its demand and not the other way round. Besides, it's also a robust application that can act as a foolproof system to any fake identities since it employs biometric identification to recognize a valid user of the system.m
